What is Neuralink?

Neuralink is a neurotechnology company developing implantable brain-computer interfaces (BCIs). These devices are designed to be surgically implanted into the brain, allowing for direct communication between the human brain and computers.

Brain-Computer Interface

Direct neural connection enabling two-way communication between brain and external devices

Neural Recording

High-resolution recording of neural activity from thousands of brain cells

Wireless Communication

Bluetooth-like connection for seamless data transmission

Research Question

Our Research Focus

Our research aims to estimate how Neuralink may impact society in the education sector and film industry as it becomes more widely used. We seek to understand the potential benefits, challenges, and societal implications of widespread adoption of brain-computer interface technology in these two critical areas.

About Our Research

Education

Exploring how Neuralink may revolutionize learning through direct brain-computer interfaces, enabling faster knowledge acquisition and enhanced cognitive capabilities, or harm learning experiences.

Cinema

Exploring how Neuralink may enhance or harm audiences of cinema by creating immersive cinematic experiences that directly stimulate the brain's visual and emotional centers.
