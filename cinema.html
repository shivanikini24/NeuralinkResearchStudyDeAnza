Our Computer Simulation

Methodology: Matched-Pairs Experiment

  • Randomly generating 1000 personas with various traits and preferences (emotional responsiveness, favorite genres, sensory sensitivity, etc)
  • Assign each persona to a randomly generated movie (based on genre, intensity, pacicng, emotional depth, etc)
  • Collect data on personas estimated experience with a traditional cinema environment
  • Collect data on personas estimated experience with a neuralink-enhanced cinema environment.

Making a Reliable Computer Simulation (Using Python)

  • Defining our sample to be a randomly selected sample of US residents aged 13+ (aligning probabilities for selection of characteristics with US population demographic)
  • Estimating learning experience with different movie types (immersion level, attention retention, enjoyment rating, etc)
  • Applied computer simulation techniques to account for natural variation in a real-time experiment (utilizing numpy)

Current Research Findings

Enjoyment Rating

Comparing the average enjoyment rating for a traditional viewing experience where a movie is one of the user's top 3 genres with a neuralink-enhanced viewing environment where the movie is not one of the user's top 3 genres, the average enjoyment rating for the neuralink-enhancedd viewing mode is higher.

Neuralink enhanced viewing appears to compensate for a genre mismatch greater than the enjoyment for regular viewing experience with a genre match.

Statistical Analysis:

  • Sample Size (Regular): 339 participants
  • Mean enjoyment rating (Regular): 7.125
  • Standard deviation (Regular): 1.341
  • Sample size (Neuralink): 661
  • Mean enjoyment rating (Neuralink): 9.046
  • Standard deviation (Neuralink): 1.583

  • p-value: 3.92 × 10⁻⁷³

Conclusion

  • The enjoyment ratings for Neuralink-enhanced without a genre match are significantly higher than for Regular viewing with a genre match. The p-value is far below 0.05, indicating this difference is extremely unlikely to be due to chance.


Adverse Effects Risk

Users are at higher risk of sensory fatigue, mild disorientation, difficulty concerntrating, headache, nausea, memory disruption, dissaociation, and panic responses.



Low Emotional Depth Movie Focus

Movies with low ratings of emotional depth still have great emotional engagement with neuralink-enhancedd viewing.

Statistical Analysis

  • 38.5% average increase in emotional engagement
  • T-statistic: 15.82 (very high significance)
  • Effect Size: Cohen's d = 1.41 (large effect)
  • 95% Confidence Interval: The true difference lies between 1.74 - 2.24 points
  • Strong correlation (r=1.00) between methods

Conclusion

  • This analysis reveals that Neuralink enhancement is particularly effective for movies with low emotional depth (< 5.0). The technology appears to compensate for the lack of natural emotional content by artificially enhancing the viewer's engagement.
